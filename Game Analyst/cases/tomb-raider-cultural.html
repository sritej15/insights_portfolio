N° 02 — Case study
The Tomb Raider franchise · Story & world

Cultural risk analysis as a milestone-cadence practice

Turning cultural sensitivity from a one-off exercise into a repeatable method that scales with production.

StudioCrystal Dynamics
RoleNarrative / player research
ScopeStory & world across development
OutcomeAdopted as milestone practice
Context

Tomb Raider is a franchise built on moving through real places and the stories that belong to them. The care taken with that material has historically been high, but the work lived in individual reviewers' heads. On a title this size, that doesn't scale.

Signal

The moments that needed the most care tended to get the least structured review. Ad-hoc check-ins with a handful of experts were producing valuable notes but inconsistently, at unpredictable points in the schedule, and often too late to change the thing being reviewed.

Method

I worked across the story and world as they came together, assessing cultural sensitivity around real regions, languages, myths, religious references, material culture, and visual shorthand. Each pass produced a short written analysis: what the current draft showed, where risk sat, and what a stronger version of the same beat could be.

The crucial shift was cadence. Instead of a single end-of-production review, the work was structured to land at each production milestone, early enough that feedback could change direction, not just polish surface. It became a scheduled input to the narrative and creative process rather than a late-stage check.

Fig 1 — Moving the cultural review from late-stage ad-hoc to scheduled inputs at every milestone.
Insight
A sensitivity read that arrives in beta can only ask for cuts. The same read in pre-production can change the story.

The content of the analysis wasn't the innovation. The innovation was treating it as an ongoing research practice tied to milestones, with a consistent written format, so the team could receive, discuss, and act on it the same way they treated any other production review.

Action & outcome
  1. Built a milestone-cadence structure for cultural risk analysis and agreed the format with narrative leadership: short written passes, consistent sections, one per milestone.
  2. Delivered the reviews across story and world development, with findings specific enough to act on and framed to support decisions rather than block them.
  3. The practice was adopted as an ongoing part of the Tomb Raider franchise's creative process, shaping how cultural sensitivity is reviewed on future Tomb Raider titles.
Secondary finding
The bigger shift was organizational, not editorial. Once the work had a schedule and a shape, other disciplines knew when to bring material in and what kind of feedback to expect back. That alone changed how early in the process the questions got asked.
