N° 03 — Case study
Battlefield Mobile · Progression sequencing

A difficulty curve running backwards

Weapon tuning looked fine in isolation. The signal was in the sequencing.

StudioEA · Industrial Toys
RolePlayer analyst · progression
ScopeFTUE → Lvl 50
OutcomeUnlock order restructured
Context

Battlefield Mobile, post-FTUE. I was driving qualitative analysis on player progression from FTUE through Lvl 50 — unlocks, rewards, pacing — alongside the weekly longitudinal playtests and the FTUE funnel work.

Signal

Performance dips kept clustering around the level gates where new weapons unlocked. Not a few points of soft churn — measurable dropoff right after the unlock, recovering a level or two later. Weapon tuning reviewed in isolation looked fine. Each weapon performed inside its target band.

Fig 1 — Session length recovered between gates and dropped at each unlock. The gates, not the weapons, were the signal.
Method

I stopped looking at weapons one at a time and started looking at the delta between adjacent unlocks. For each gate, I paired the telemetry dip with longitudinal playtest notes from players who had just crossed it: what they had just been using, what they unlocked, how it felt relative to the weapon it replaced.

The pattern was consistent. At several gates, the freshly unlocked weapon required a different playstyle than the one players had spent the previous ten levels mastering — close-range to mid-range, ADS-dominant to hip-fire, single-target to area. Each weapon was balanced. The transition was not. Players who had just internalized a weapon were being asked to re-learn combat at the exact moment the game was signaling progress.

Insight
Progression had been designed weapon by weapon. Players experienced it gap by gap.

The difficulty curve wasn't running forwards with occasional spikes. At the gates, it was running backwards: skill acquired at Lvl N was partially obsolete at Lvl N+1. That's why tuning each weapon harder or softer never fixed the dip — the dip was in the seam, not the node.

Action & outcome
  1. Reframed the review from per-weapon balance to unlock-sequence balance, with telemetry dips mapped against playstyle deltas at each gate.
  2. Partnered with design and PM to restructure unlock ordering: weapons that reinforced the previous playstyle moved earlier in the sequence, and high-delta weapons were paired with transitional unlocks — attachments, modes, or mechanics that bridged one playstyle to the next.
  3. The sequencing shipped into the progression funnel work. Combined with the FTUE changes, the full progression stack improved completion rate through the early-to-mid level band and flattened the dip at the restructured gates.
What this reinforced
When a signal keeps clustering, the explanation is probably one level of abstraction higher than where you're looking. The gates were signal. The weapons were noise.
